Food Prep!

This should be done before the method.

To ease the process.
  • Get the butter out beforehand so it can warm up to room temperature, it will be easier to work with.

Let's get to the Method!‚ú®

  1. Firstly, chop up the butter into chunks.
  2. Secondly, sift the flour the into your mixing bowl and add the salt.
  3. Then, put the bowl in the fridge for a few minutes to chill (keeping ingredients cold is a way of ensuring the butter does not coat the grain making the pastry unable to soak up the water we need to add later) .
  4. Once we have cold ingredients we can add the butter to the bowl and stir it so that every piece of butter is coated in flour! ‚ù§
  5. We now need to use our hands and work the pastry a bit with our fingers (this process favours those with cold hands) , you will know it is ready when it has reached a breadcrumb like appearance.
  6. Now we can add our water, pour 100ml then add 10ml at a time, incorporating with a fork, to form a rough dough.
  7. Then, bring the dough together into a single ball and heavily dust the surface you are working on and your hands in flour.
  8. turn the dough onto the floured surface and roll the dough into a fat tube, without applying too much pressure wrap it up in cling film and refrigerate for 15 minutes.
  9. lightly reflour the surface and roll out the pastry until it is about as thick as a ¬£1 coin and 3 times as long as it is wide, keep tossing the pastry as you roll to ensure an even rectangular roll and squish the sides in as necessary.
  10. Fold the bottom and top third of the pastry in on itself, to make a square block.
  11. Fold the pastry in half again and press the edges together with the rolling pin.
  12. Roll out and fold the pastry again, repeating steps 9-11 four times.If the pastry feels greasy at any point, or starts to spring back when as you roll, then cover and chill it for 10 minutes before continuing.
  13. Chill the finished pastry for atleast an hour, or ideally overnight, before using.
  14. When you use the pastry roll it out to about the thickness of a ¬£1 coin, and cook it at 200C/400F/Gas 6 for around 30 minutes or until it is golden.
  15. Serves: 550g puff pastry.
